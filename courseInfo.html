Class Info & Resources

5 Credits, Grades 9-12 This course provides an overview of technology's impact on society, including its historical context, current effects, and potential future developments. Unit 1 of Technological Systems and Design provides an overview of technology's impact on society, including its historical context, current effects, and potential future developments. Unit 2: Design and Reverse Engineering explores the process of design and reverse engineering. Unit 3â€™s Technical Literacy covers the fundamentals of sketching and technical drawing. During Unit 4 Structures, students will be introduced to different types of forces and structural members. Unit 5: Mechanisms introduces and experiments with various mechanical forces, materials and mechanisms. During Unit 6 Power and Energy, students will learn about and experiment with various forms of mechanical, electrical and fluid energy forms. Finally, in Unit 7 students will demonstrate that they can apply the design/ engineering process in a practical and/ or theoretical situation in "capstone-like" projects.

The class is project based, each unit has a major project associated with it. Utilizes hands on skills and techniques to achieve the goal for each project.

Tech Systems is essential for preparing students for a digital-driven workforce, equipping them with critical skills . As technology continues to evolve, early exposure will foster innovation and problem-solving abilities, ensuring students remain competitive in an increasingly automated world. Additionally, integrating tech education will bridge the digital divide.
